Leadership

Ed is a self-employed software engineer and 15-year resident, living in the City of Sheboygan Falls with his wife and children. He regularly enjoys officiating high school sports, playing ultimate frisbee and volleyball, and joining his kids in all things backyard.

  • Currently on school board, standing boldly for our students
  • Professional experience managing teams in large corporations
  • Active in the PTA, Middle School Robotics, Junior Achievement, and Special Olympics

Ed sees his role as a steward, prudently investing taxpayer funds into preparing each student for a lifetime ahead, founded on solid skills, character, and wisdom.

Initiatives

Ed has supported several initiatives to improve our schools while remaining careful with spending. Here are some highlights. Those adopted by the district are marked with a ✅.

Safe students

Protocols: Train teachers in a wider range of responses to threats. ✅

Security: Modernize cameras and other technology. ✅

Private spaces: Give parents confidence that girls’ bathrooms, locker rooms, and sports teams are separate from boys.

Strong academics

Best courses: Expand our college-level offerings – an incredible value for students and taxpayers. ✅ (with more to do)

Best curriculums: New evidence-based math and language curriculums. Verify no CRT or other indoctrination. ✅

Best standards: Cut the liberal ideology from DPI’s academic standards and bring back our heritage.

Best teachers: Merit pay attracts and retains the best teachers at fair market rates.

Classrooms without politics

Neutrality: Remove LGBT and other political flags and Safe Space signs from our classrooms. Classrooms should be safe, welcoming, and free from divisive ideology.

Academic depth: Encourage multi-perspective, critical thinking – not one-sided slogans – within the classroom walls.

Engaged community

Transparency: Publicly post instructional material online. Letting the community in keeps special interests out.

Input: Put all sex-ed instruction before a citizen committee and the Board. End the Gender and Sexuality Alliance side channel.

Awareness: Ensure the community is informed about important decisions, such as the LGBT-activist-drafted sex ed standards dropped after public outcry.
